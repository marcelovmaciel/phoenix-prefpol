#+date: 2026-03-30 13:14:57

* Scope
- Julia version used: =1.11.9=
- Years analyzed: =2006, 2018, 2022=
- Bootstraps per year: =3=
- Imputation variants: =zero= and =mice=
- Pipeline path: exact cached path with candidate-set cache, disk bootstrap, disk imputation, streamed weak profiles, and streamed linearization.
- This report supersedes the earlier quick 2022 exploratory scan. The counts below come from the exact cached pipeline path.

* Main Findings
- Ties are real in all three years: 772 tied group consensuses out of 9076 total evaluations (8.51% overall).
- Highest annual tie rate: 2022 with 275 / 2112 tied (13.02%).
- Lowest annual tie rate: 2006 with 114 / 1780 tied (6.40%).
- Tie rates rise with candidate-set size. The highest observed m-level is m=7 at 21.31%; the lowest is m=2 at 0.56%.
- The most tie-prone year-scenario slice is 2022 / lula_bolsonaro at 13.02%.
- The most tie-prone year-demographic slice is 2022 / Religion at 24.77%, with max minimizer multiplicity 504.
- Ties are not only a tiny-group artifact. Some of the largest tied groups have masses in the hundreds, although the most extreme minimizer multiplicities occur in very small groups.
- Substantive implication: =C= is unchanged by tie choice because it depends only on the minimized objective, but =D= is tie-rule dependent.
- Production tie-breaking now uses =deterministic_pseudorandom_minimizer=. The representative consensus is selected deterministically from the full minimizing set using stable tie-break metadata when supplied, or otherwise the exact active ordered candidate tuple plus the compressed ballot multiset.
- The full minimizing set is retained on each =ConsensusResult= via =all_minimizers= for diagnostics and sensitivity analysis.

* Notes
- The slow stage in the all-years run was still R/=mice= imputation, not the brute-force Kendall consensus search.
- The most extreme tie count in this run is 504 minimizers for a 2022 religion group with group mass 2 in the =zero= variant at m=7.
- 2022 shows the strongest tie pressure overall, especially in Religion and Race, and at larger m values.
- 2018 also has substantial tie incidence, especially Religion at 17.50%.
- 2006 has the lowest overall tie rate, but ties are still present and become nontrivial by m=6.
